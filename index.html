Purpose of the Study

This research intends to determine whether different types of phishing awareness training, based on indicators, have an effect on the likelihood of an individual falling for a phishing attack.

Why have I been chosen?

You are part of the demographic that this study is targeted towards, and a diverse set of individuals is required for this study.

Do I have to take part?

It is entirely up to you to decide whether or not to take part. If you decide to take part you will be asked to confirm your agreement via a consent form on the following page. You may withdraw at any time before clicking the final submit button.

What will happen if I take part?
  • Watch an approximately 5-minute training video that explains how to identify phishing emails.
  • Complete a simulation where you will be shown a number of different emails.
  • Make decisions about whether each email is phishing or legitimate.
  • Indicate your confidence level in each answer.
Data Collected
  • Whether you identified each email correctly.
  • Your confidence level for each decision.
  • Time taken to submit each answer.
  • Your age range.

No personally identifiable information will be collected. Since any data you submit cannot identify you, deleting your specific simulation data is not technically feasible — you cannot be identified within the dataset. You may withdraw at any time during the simulation; no unsubmitted data will be recorded.

Possible Risks

There are no disadvantages or risks foreseen in taking part in this study.

Possible Benefits

By taking part you will be contributing to the improvement of phishing training and will also be able to improve your own phishing detection skills through the training and practice provided during the simulation.

Confidentiality

No personal information will be collected. All other information collected from your submission will be kept strictly confidential and accessible only to the researcher and supervising mentor. Data may be retained and published in anonymised form. Results will not be attributed to you in any identifiable way.

Step 2 of 3 — Instructions

Email assessment task

You will now review 10 emails, one at a time. For each email, follow these steps:

  • Read the email as you normally would if it appeared in your inbox
  • Decide whether it is phishing (suspicious, fraudulent) or legitimate (a genuine email), then click the corresponding button.
  • Rate your confidence in that decision on a scale from 1 (not confident at all) to 5 (very confident).
  • Click Next Email to move on. You cannot go back to a previous email.

⏱ There is no time limit. Your decision speed is recorded automatically in the background, take as long as you naturally would.

Study Debrief

This study compared two types of phishing awareness training. One type teaches broad indicators which are cues present in almost all phishing emails, such as suspicious sender addresses and a sense of urgency. The other teaches both broad and specific indicators — including cues like misspellings and distorted logos that appear in only some phishing emails.

Your training was randomly assigned at the start of the session. The aim of the research is to determine which approach better equips people to detect phishing emails, especially sophisticated ones that lack the obvious specific cues.
